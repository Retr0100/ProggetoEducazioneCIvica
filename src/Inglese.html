Solar Energy in the World

What is solar energy?

Solar energy is the energy we get from sunlight. You can think of it as a battery that recharges every time the sun shines on it. This energy is collected by solar panels, which are made of many small solar cells that capture sunlight and turn it into electricity. This electricity can be used to power our homes, schools and offices, as well as to charge our cell phones and electric cars. Solar energy is clean, renewable energy, which means we produce no pollutants and don't worry about it running out like oil or gas.

The energy in the world

There are several countries that are making great progress in the use of solar energy such as: Germany, China, the United States , India and Japan

Japan

Japan is one of the most active countries in the use of solar energy, with an installed capacity of more than 70 GW. Solar energy accounts for about 7 percent of the country's total energy mix, and the Japanese government has adopted incentive policies to promote the use of solar energy, such as the Feed-in Tariff (FIT) program. Japan has also introduced building codes that promote the use of solar panels on the roofs of buildings.

China

China is the world leader in solar panel production and has invested heavily in the use of solar energy. In 2020, China surpassed the United States to become the country with the largest installed solar capacity, with more than 240 GW. Solar energy accounts for about 5 percent of the country's total energy mix, and China has adopted incentive policies to promote the use of solar energy, such as the Top Runner program, which incentivizes the development of innovative and high-efficiency technologies.

Germany

Germany is one of the world leaders in the use of solar energy, with an installed capacity of more than 50 GW. Solar energy accounts for about 8 percent of the country's total energy mix and 25 percent of electricity generated during daylight hours. Germany has adopted government policies and incentives to promote the use of solar energy, such as the Feed-in Tariff (FIT) program, which guarantees a fixed price for solar energy produced by domestic and commercial installations.

United States of America

the United States is one of the world's largest consumers of energy, but in recent years it has made great progress in the use of solar energy. In 2020, the United States installed about 19 GW of new solar capacity, bringing total installed capacity to more than 97 GW. Solar energy accounts for about 3 percent of the country's total energy mix, and the United States has adopted incentive policies to promote the use of solar energy, such as the Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC), which offers tax credits for the installation of solar systems.

India

India is making great progress in the use of solar energy, with an installed capacity of more than 40 GW. Solar energy accounts for about 4 percent of the country's total energy mix, and the Indian government has adopted incentive policies to promote the use of solar energy, such as the Jawaharlal Nehru National Solar Mission program, which aims to install 100 GW of solar capacity by 2022.
